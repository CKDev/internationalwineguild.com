Level I: Register for the Guild Wine Seminar

This introductory course is designed for wine enthusiasts as well as wine and food service professionals who want to deepen their knowledge of wine. Taste 26 wines on your way to developing a basic understanding of Old and New World Wine, including lectures that cover history, terminology, and technical wine evaluation. 

Participants have the option of earning one of 5 certifications upon successful completion of this seminar and its online certification exam: 

  • Professional Diplomas: Wine Sommelier, Wine Manager, Wine Merchant, Chef of Wine Arts (must already have been granted the title of Chef)
  • Enthusiast (non-professional) Diploma: Wine Cellar Manager

This seminar is taught each month. Register early to reserve your space! Click a corresponding "Register" link to sign up. The cost of the seminar is $575 (includes wine, gourmet lunch, educational materials and instruction).

Registration & Cancellation Policy:

You must register for courses through the International Wine and Spirits Guild. Online registration closes 1 day prior to class start date. If you need to register after the close date, please contact the International Wine and Spirits Guild at 303-296-3966.

Because we purchase wines and food based on the number of registrants, no-shows are charged a non-refundable fee equal to $525 per person, unless other arrangements are made at least 7 working days in advance of the seminar.

If for any reason a seminar is canceled we will phone or e-mail you 48 hours prior to the start of the seminar. If a seminar is canceled at the last minute due to bad weather (this is very rare) we will reschedule the seminar. However, if a seminar is held and you decide not to attend because you believe the weather is bad then it will be treated as a no-show.

It is highly recommended that you register early, your credit card will not be charged until ten days in advance of the course. We must have a minimum students registered in order to form a course. If you are traveling from out of town to attend, please do not make travel arrangements until two weeks in advance of the seminar or course.
